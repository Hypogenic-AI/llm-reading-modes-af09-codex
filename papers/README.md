# Downloaded Papers

1. [A Survey on LLM-Generated Text Detection: Necessity, Methods, and Future Directions](2310.14724_wu_2023_llm_generated_text_detection_survey.pdf)
   - Authors: Junchao Wu, Shu Yang, Runzhe Zhan, Yulin Yuan, Derek Fai Wong, Lidia Sam Chao
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: `2310.14724`
   - Why relevant: Broad survey of detector families, datasets, evaluation challenges, and open problems for mode detection in text.

2. [SemEval-2024 Task 8: Multidomain, Multimodel and Multilingual Machine-Generated Text Detection](2404.14183_wang_2024_semeval_task8_mgt_detection.pdf)
   - Authors: Yuxia Wang et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2404.14183`
   - Why relevant: Strong benchmark paper covering binary detection, generator attribution, and human-machine boundary detection.

3. [LLM-DetectAIve: a Tool for Fine-Grained Machine-Generated Text Detection](2408.04284_abassy_2024_llm_detectaive.pdf)
   - Authors: Mervat Abassy et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2408.04284`
   - Why relevant: Extends binary detection into four classes, including machine-humanized and machine-polished text.

4. [Detecting Machine-Generated Texts: Not Just "AI vs Humans" and Explainability is Complicated](2406.18259_ji_2024_not_just_ai_vs_humans.pdf)
   - Authors: Jiazhou Ji et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2406.18259`
   - Why relevant: Argues for an explicit "undecided" class and emphasizes explainability rather than forced binary decisions.

5. [TEXTMACHINA: Seamless Generation of Machine-Generated Text Datasets](2401.03946_sarvazyan_2024_textmachina.pdf)
   - Authors: Areg Mikael Sarvazyan, Jose Angel Gonzalez, Marc Franco-Salvador
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2401.03946`
   - Why relevant: Practical framework for constructing datasets for detection, attribution, boundary, and mixcase tasks.

6. [Source Attribution for Large Language Model-Generated Data](2310.00646_wang_2023_source_attribution_llm_generated_data.pdf)
   - Authors: Jingtan Wang et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: `2310.00646`
   - Why relevant: Moves from "was it AI?" to "which source/provider?" and frames attribution as a structured signal.

7. [Keystroke Dynamics Against Academic Dishonesty in the Age of LLMs](2406.15335_kundu_2024_keystroke_dynamics_llm_dishonesty.pdf)
   - Authors: Debnath Kundu et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2406.15335`
   - Why relevant: Shows a non-textual behavioral mode signal that is highly predictive of AI assistance.

8. [Recording for Eyes, Not Echoing to Ears: Contextualized Spoken-to-Written Conversion of ASR Transcripts](2408.09688_liu_2024_spoken_to_written_asr_transcripts.pdf)
   - Authors: Jiaqing Liu et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: `2408.09688`
   - Why relevant: Useful proxy literature for the "dictated/spoken vs written" mode, with a dedicated spoken-to-written benchmark.
