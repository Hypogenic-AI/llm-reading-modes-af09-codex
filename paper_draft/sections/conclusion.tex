\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We study whether LLMs detect production modes beyond binary authorship and whether those modes behave like latent variables in representation space. Using a controlled six-mode corpus with matched \hcthree anchors, we find that \gptfourone perfectly separates broad production modes, substantially outperforms a grouped stylometric baseline, and leaves measurable mode structure in \embeddingmodel embeddings. At the same time, the same prompting setup performs poorly on external six-way source attribution in \semeval Subtask B.

The key takeaway is straightforward: LLMs read broad process modes well, but zero-shot generator identity remains much harder.

The next step is to make this claim harder to satisfy. Future work should decouple generator and judge model families, replace synthetic proxies with real dictated-versus-typed and keyboard-derived data, add abstention and ambiguity labels for borderline cases, and test whether the learned mode directions transfer to mixed-authorship benchmarks such as \semeval Subtask C.
